# PLAN.MD — Pluckk-Inspired Veg & Fruits E-Commerce Redesign

## 1. Project Overview

### Project Name

Pluckk-Inspired Fresh Produce E-Commerce Website Redesign

### Project Type

E-commerce website + mobile-first shopping experience for fruits, vegetables, clean-label food, meal kits, and health-led fresh products.

### Main Goal

Redesign the existing Pluckk-style fresh produce website into a more user-friendly, aesthetic, premium, and conversion-focused e-commerce experience.

The new experience should help users quickly understand the brand, trust the product quality, browse by need, search easily, add items faster, and complete checkout with minimum friction.

---

## 2. Source Context Used

### Uploaded Design Reference

The uploaded `pluckk.in-DESIGN.md` defines a fresh, wholesome, farm-inspired design system with:

- Forest green, deep teal, mint, magenta, white, and soft neutral colors
- Montserrat for headings and DM Sans for body/UI text
- Clean product cards with subtle shadows

- Generous whitespace and mobile-first layouts
- Organic, premium, health-conscious visual direction

### Live Site Context

The official Pluckk website currently includes:

- App download prompt

- Search, account, cart, and help controls
- Delivery messaging

## 3. Redesign North Star

### Core Experience Statement

Make Pluckk feel like a premium fresh-food shopping app on the web: clean, fast, trustworthy, healthy, and effortless.

### What the User Should Feel

- “This food is fresh and safe.”
- “I can quickly find what I need.”
- “This brand feels premium, but not complicated.”
- “I trust the quality before adding to cart.”
- “Checkout feels smooth and reliable.”

### What the Business Should Achieve

- Higher add-to-cart rate
- Better product discovery
- Higher repeat purchases
- More app downloads without hurting web shopping
- Stronger trust around freshness, safety, traceability, and quality control
- Better mobile conversion
- Lower user confusion on category/product pages

## 5. Key Redesign Problems to Solve

### 5.1 Homepage Clarity

Current homepage has many sections, but the user should instantly understand:

- What Pluckk sells
- Why it is better than normal produce shopping
- How quickly they can get it
- What categories they can shop
- What trust proof supports the quality promise

### 5.2 Product Discovery

Fresh produce users do not always shop by category only. They also shop by:

- Daily essentials
- Health goals
- Recipes
- Meal prep
- Kids/family needs
- Seasonal produce
- Offers
- Repeat purchases

The redesign should support all these browsing behaviors.

### 5.3 Product Card Conversion

Product cards should make buying decisions easier by showing:

- Product image
- Product name
- Quality tag: Ozone-Washed / Traceable / Clean Label / Freshly Cut
- Quantity options
- Price
- Discount, if available
- Delivery estimate or freshness badge
- Clear Add button

### 5.4 Trust Communication

Trust should not be hidden in separate pages only. Quality proof should appear across the shopping journey:

- Homepage
- Product listing pages
- Product detail pages
- Cart
- Checkout

### 5.5 Mobile Shopping Speed

Since grocery buying is frequent and repetitive, the mobile experience must be faster than desktop:

- Sticky search
- Sticky cart
- Bottom navigation
- Fast Add button
- Compact filters
- One-hand friendly UI

## 12. Accessibility Plan

### Requirements

- Minimum body text: 14px
- Preferred body text: 16px
- Touch target: minimum 44px on mobile
- Clear keyboard focus states
- Sufficient contrast for text and buttons
- Alt text for produce images
- Labels for form fields
- ARIA labels for cart, search, and filter controls
- Avoid color-only communication for status badges

### Focus States

Use mint outline or shadow:

```css
box-shadow: rgba(149, 232, 190, 0.3) 0px 0px 0px 3px;
border: 1px solid #95e8be;
```

## 16. Visual Design Execution Rules

### Do

- Use large clean product images.
- Keep category cards simple.
- Use mint for primary actions.
- Use forest green for trust and headings.
- Use magenta only for high-priority accents/offers.
- Keep white space generous.
- Make buttons consistent.
- Keep mobile shopping fast.

### Do Not

- Do not overload the homepage with too many equal-weight banners.
- Do not hide search.
- Do not make app download compete with shopping.
- Do not use tiny product card text.
- Do not use heavy shadows.
- Do not overuse magenta.
- Do not make filters difficult to access on mobile.
- Do not bury trust proof only in footer pages.
